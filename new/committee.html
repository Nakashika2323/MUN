Specialized Agencies

The Organization of Petroleum Exporting Nations, the Press Corps, and the House of Representatives. These committees are completely different in terms of procedure, or in other words, they follow different rules. The press corps will be made up of reporters and journalists whereas HOR will be a strictly Nepali speaking committee.

The Organization of Petroleum Exporting Countries (OPEC) was founded in Iraq, Baghdad in September 1960 (10-14) and has five founding members: the Islamic Republic of Iran, Iraq, Kuwait, Saudi Arabia and Venezuela. There are 13 groups, the world's largest oil exporters. It provides its members with technical and financial assistance. OPEC members coordinate policies on oil prices, production, and related matters semi-annually and at special OPEC meetings. It has about four-fifths of the world's proven oil reserves. OPEC members are discussing issues related to oil prices, crises and other burning issues.

In the press corps committee the delegates become journalists and reporters instead of debating issues and representing countries or characters. Press corps is a group of journalists and reporters who are all working in different settings. They work for different newspapers, cover different areas and prepare articles based on the actual chain of events that occurred in their allocated areas from the points they collected during the event. They are designed to represent the group of journalists present at the UN and other important bodies. House of Representatives

Regional Bodies

Regional Bodies have some of the most interesting committees to sign up for due to the sheer amount of variation between them. Regional organizations serve the United Nations' mission in different parts of the world by providing a culturally sensitive and geographically oriented platform for discussing important issues.

The League of Arab Nations (LAN) is an intergovernmental pan-Arab organization of all Arab countries in the Middle East and North Africa, founded in Cairo on March 22, 1945. The league currently has 22 members. Aiming to be a regional organization of Arab countries focused on economic development, conflict resolution and coordination of political goals

is a regional organization consisting of seven member countries located on the coast and adjacent areas of the Bay of Bengal, forming a cohesive regional organization. There are seven member countries, five from South Asia including Bangladesh, Bhutan, India, Nepal and Sri Lanka, and two from Southeast Asia including Myanmar and Thailand. Their purpose is to create favorable conditions for rapid economic development by identifying and implementing specific cooperative projects and supporting each other.

The Organization of American States is the oldest regional organization in the world. The organization is made up of 35 independent American states and forms the most important political, legal and social government forum in the hemisphere. The four main pillars of the organization, democracy, human rights, security and development, support and intertwine with each other through political dialogue, inclusion, cooperation, legal and follow-up means, and work in the OAS in the hemisphere.






Crisis Committees

The Crisis Committees are the highly challenging and most intense committees at GEMSMUN. The procedures are totally different and topics within the committees rapidly change. These committees will experience continous intervention from the secretariat and delegates must be able to adapt themselves to the dynamic nature of the committee. The crisis committees will be interconnected with one committee influencing the business of another committee or committees. Each committees would have 10 to a maximum of 12 delegates.

he North Atlantic Treaty Organization (NATO) was created in 1949 and is a group of 30 European and North American countries that exists to protect its people and territory. NATO russia council, also known as NRC was established at NATO Russia Submit in Rome. The NRC is a collaboration, joint decision, and joint action framework in which individual NATO member states and Russia collaborate as equal partners on a wide range of security matters of mutual interest. They work on topics ranging from counter-narcotics and counter-terrorism.

The International Criminal Police Organization, also known as INTERPOL is the world's largest international police organization. The role is to enable police forces all around the world to work together in order to make the world a safer place. During the sessions, different countries met to discuss different procedures and technologies related to the capturing of criminals.

One of the most significant parts of the UN is the Security council. The Closed Door Historic Security Council, as its name implies, is a body that examines historical events and considers how events may have gone differently. The committee's findings are shielded from application in the current world chronology by the closed door in the name. Being a body that analyzes historical topics, the council engages in discussions and disputes that are unmatched by any other committee.
